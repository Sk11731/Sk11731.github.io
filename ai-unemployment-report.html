Addressing Mass Unemployment Due to AI Automation

01 — The Problem

The rapid adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) and autonomous agents has shifted the labor market landscape. ILO projections suggest over 2 billion informal workers face displacement. This research evaluates the efficacy of policy interventions in mitigating economic volatility.

02 — Methodology

Our approach involved a longitudinal simulation of labor-market integration. We modeled three distinct policy archetypes across four key sectors: Manufacturing, Data Entry, Legal Services, and Creative Arts. Metrics focused on re-entry speed and long-term income stabilization.

04 — Key Trade-offs

While AI Literacy programs are highly scalable, they fail to address the fundamental lack of demand for displaced skill sets. Conversely, targeted Reskilling requires significant upfront government investment but provides the most robust path toward sustained economic utility.

05 — Recommendation

Conclusion: We recommend a phased transition toward sector-specific, government-backed reskilling programs. Priority should be given to mid-career workers whose skills are most vulnerable to automation but have the highest potential for cross-domain application.
