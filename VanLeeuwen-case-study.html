Van Leeuwen: Arabic Coffee Toffee

A Market Entry Strategy for Doha, Qatar

01 — Brand Philosophy

Founded in 2008 in NYC by Ben Van Leeuwen and Laura O'Neill, the company began with a simple mission: to provide ice cream that makes you feel good. By prioritizing clean labels, ethically-sourced ingredients, and constant innovation, Van Leeuwen has transitioned from a two-truck operation to a cross-country premium brand. This expansion into Qatar leverages the brand's core competency—premium quality—while localizing the product offering through a culturally resonant flavor profile.

02 — The Qatar Market Opportunity

Qatar represents a unique geography for the brand's first global Arabic-flavor launch. The local market for premium food products has seen a 6.7% CAGR. Our flagship product—a blend of Baladna dairy and organic Souq Waqif Arabic coffee—is designed to bridge the gap between traditional Qahwa rituals and modern frozen desserts. While competitors like Häagen-Dazs maintain dominance in the standard market, they lack the cultural specificity required to capture the local premium segment.

03 — Customer Segmentation & Targeting

Qatar's demographic is unique, with 88.4% of residents being foreign expatriates and 99.7% of the population living in urban areas. We have segmented our potential market into two primary tiers:

  • Experience Seekers: Located in The Pearl, West Bay, and Lusail. These 22–40-year-old professionals prioritize novelty and premium quality, with a monthly income exceeding 25,000 QAR.
  • Traditional Gift Buyers: Found near high-traffic retail hubs like Villaggio and Place Vendôme. Aged 30–55, this segment values tradition and hospitality, often purchasing luxury food items as part of seasonal cultural exchanges.

04 — Operational Logistics & Risk Mitigation

The operational environment in Qatar presents significant challenges, primarily regarding the cold chain. With summer temperatures frequently exceeding 45°C, maintaining a stable -18°C throughout the supply chain is critical.

Strategic Risk Analysis:

  • Supply Chain Dependency: We have partnered with JSL Global Qatar to ensure end-to-end freezer truck coverage. This removes the need for capital-intensive warehouse construction.
  • Regulatory Compliance: We adhere strictly to GSO labeling laws. Every pint is bilingual (Arabic/English), featuring MoPH halal certification, ensuring zero friction at customs.

05 — Financial Outlook & KPIs

The retail strategy is built around a premium price point of 36 QAR per pint, with a contribution margin of 16 QAR per unit. Our year-one financial objectives include:

  • Break-even point: Achieving 33,125 units sold within the first 12 months.
  • Brand Awareness: Reaching a 35% brand awareness rate in our core urban segments (The Pearl, West Bay, Lusail).
  • Growth Target: A projected 20% increase in sales revenue by the end of Month 12, driven by targeted Ramadan and Eid marketing bundles.

06 — Rollout Roadmap

The strategy follows a four-phase implementation:

  • Months 1-2 (Pre-Launch): Securing supply lines and packaging finalization.
  • Month 3 (Launch): High-visibility event in Souq Waqif and digital activation.
  • Months 4-9 (Growth): Sustained Instagram spending and seasonal gifting promotions.
  • Months 10-12 (Evaluation): Performance review against our 20% revenue growth target.
