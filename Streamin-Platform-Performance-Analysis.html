Streaming Platform Performance

A 5-Year Comparative Analysis (2021–2025)

01 — Research Methodology & Scope

This analysis, documented in the COMP1401_Final_Presentation.pptx, evaluates the financial and subscriber trajectories of Netflix, Disney+, and MBC Shahid[cite: 2]. By utilizing data drawn from official annual filings and investor relations reports aggregated in the COMP1401_Final_Excel.xlsx, we mapped 75 distinct data points to decode the strategies underpinning industry sustainability[cite: 2].

The research methodology involved calculating year-over-year fluctuations in revenue, profit, and total subscribers[cite: 2]. Furthermore, we analyzed the demographic ratio of users (under-18 vs. 18+) to identify the structural foundations of each platform's revenue base[cite: 2].

02 — Financial & Subscriber Snapshots (2025)

The market positions of the three platforms as of 2025 reveal significant operational divergence[cite: 2]:

  • Netflix: Continues to lead with 325M total subscribers and a commanding 70% market share[cite: 2]. The platform generated $45.2B in revenue and a net profit of $10.2B[cite: 2].
  • Disney+: Holding a 28% market share with 132M subscribers, the platform achieved its first real profit of $0.45B in 2025 following a volatile period of heavy investment[cite: 2].
  • MBC Shahid: With a 1.4% market share (6.4M subscribers), the regional platform demonstrated significant growth, seeing a 236% increase in revenue since 2021 and approaching near-breakeven status with a minimal loss of $0.01B in 2025[cite: 2].

03 — Critical Strategic Findings

The data from the COMP1401_Final_Excel.xlsx provides several counter-intuitive insights into the streaming industry[cite: 2]:

  • The Subscriber-Profit Fallacy: Subscriber volume does not equate to financial health; Disney+ reached its peak of 164.2M subscribers in 2022, the same year it suffered its worst financial loss of $4.0B[cite: 2].
  • The 2022 Inflection Point: 2022 served as an industry-wide stress test, where rising content costs and market saturation forced all three platforms to re-evaluate their business models[cite: 2].
  • Adult-Base Stability: Netflix’s consistent revenue foundation is attributed to its 77–79% adult (18+) subscriber share, whereas faster growth for younger demographics was recorded at Disney+ (42%) and Shahid (33%)[cite: 2].
  • Specialization as a Path: MBC Shahid’s success proves that a regional focus is a viable path to financial sustainability without needing to compete on a global scale[cite: 2].

04 — Conclusion & Implications

The primary conclusion is that discipline in content spending and revenue-per-user optimization drives long-term profitability more effectively than raw subscriber acquisition[cite: 2]. Netflix's sustained profitability, Disney+'s turnaround, and MBC Shahid's regional efficiency all indicate that 2022 was the industry's defining inflection point, post which platforms had to adapt to more rigorous financial expectations[cite: 2].
